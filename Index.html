Biology Distinctions

NCERT Class 12 • MP Board Format • High Yield Topics

🦕 Convergent vs. Divergent Evolution
Parameter Convergent Evolution Divergent Evolution
Origin Different ancestral origin (Unrelated species). Common ancestral origin (Related species).
Organs Involved Results in Analogous organs. Results in Homologous organs.
Function Structures perform the same function. Structures perform different functions.
Cause Driven by similar environmental needs. Driven by adaptation to different environments (Adaptive Radiation).
Example Wings of Butterfly and Birds. Forelimbs of Whale, Bat, and Human.
🧬 Template Strand vs. Coding Strand
Parameter Template Strand Coding Strand
Polarity It has polarity \( 3' \rightarrow 5' \). It has polarity \( 5' \rightarrow 3' \).
Role Acts as a template for RNA synthesis. Does not code for RNA; acts as a reference point.
Transcription Transcription actually occurs on this strand. Transcription does not occur on this strand.
Sequence Sequence is complementary to mRNA. Sequence is same as mRNA (except Thymine is replaced by Uracil in mRNA).
Other Name Also called Antisense or Non-coding strand. Also called Sense strand.
🔬 mRNA vs. tRNA
Parameter mRNA (Messenger RNA) tRNA (Transfer RNA)
Shape Linear structure. Clover-leaf shape (2D) or Inverted L-shape (3D).
Function Carries genetic information from DNA to Ribosome. Carries specific amino acids to the Ribosome.
Genetic Unit Contains Codons. Contains Anticodons.
Molecular Weight High molecular weight (longest RNA). Low molecular weight (smallest RNA).
Stability Least stable (degrades after translation). More stable and reusable.
🩸 Repetitive DNA vs. Satellite DNA
Parameter Repetitive DNA Satellite DNA
Definition DNA sequences that are repeated many times in the genome. Highly repetitive DNA that separates as a distinct peak during centrifugation.
Classification It is a broad category including satellite DNA. It is a specific type of repetitive DNA.
Coding Status Some parts may code for proteins (rarely). Strictly non-coding.
Separation Does not necessarily form a separate band in density gradient. Forms a minor peak (Satellite) separate from bulk genomic DNA.
Function Structural role and gene expression regulation. Basis of DNA Fingerprinting (Polymorphism).
🌱 Homozygous vs. Heterozygous
Parameter Homozygous Heterozygous
Alleles Organism has similar alleles for a trait (e.g., TT or tt). Organism has dissimilar alleles for a trait (e.g., Tt).
Purity It is pure for a particular trait. It is a hybrid organism.
Gametes Produces only one type of gamete. Produces two different types of gametes.
Self-Breeding Produces identical offspring upon self-breeding. Produces mixed offspring in F2 generation.
Vigor Usually less vigorous. Shows Hybrid Vigor (Heterosis).
❌ Monohybrid vs. Dihybrid Cross
Parameter Monohybrid Cross Dihybrid Cross
Definition Cross involving inheritance of only one character. Cross involving inheritance of two characters simultaneously.
Pairs of Genes One pair of contrasting genes involved. Two pairs of contrasting genes involved.
Phenotypic Ratio F2 Phenotypic ratio is 3:1. F2 Phenotypic ratio is 9:3:3:1.
Genotypic Ratio F2 Genotypic ratio is 1:2:1. F2 Genotypic ratio is 1:2:1:2:4:2:1:2:1.
Law Proved Explains Law of Dominance & Law of Segregation. Explains Law of Independent Assortment.
